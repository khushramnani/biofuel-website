What is Biodiesel?

Biodiesel is an alternative form of fossil fuel, and it is also known as a clean energy source. It is a renewable substitute for petroleum and safer to handle. Biodiesel is produced from Eco Green Raw Material. The process of converting these raw materials into oils and then into biodiesel is known as transesterification. This biodiesel can be used in engines and vehicles.

Biodiesel Blends

Biodiesel can easily be blended with petroleum in all proportions. There are mainly four types of trending blends of biodiesel. They are listed below:
  • 100% biodiesel, B100
  • 50% biodiesel, B50
  • 20% biodiesel, B20
  • 5% biodiesel, B5
They all are hydrocarbon-based diesel. B50 and lower blends can be used in diesel equipment with only minor modifications. However, B100 does not require any modification. When we use Eco Green Raw Material, we only produce B100 Blend Biodiesel. It also reduces other toxic emissions and could increase nitrogen oxide emissions.

Advantages of Biodiesel

  • Biodiesel is not only biodegradable but also a non-toxic fuel.
  • Its formation is from renewable resources.
  • Biodiesel reduces the need for foreign fuel(ex- Petroleum).
  • It can be grown, produced, and distributed locally.
  • It improves the fuel economy.
  • Biodiesel helps to remove the unwanted particles of air and improve its quality.
  • It is used as a cleaner for biofuel refineries.

BioDiesel: Best Way To Support Go Green India

Biodiesel is a good alternative for petroleum fuels. It contributes to an important role in going green in India. Biodiesel is made from Eco Green Raw Material and is a source of renewable energy.

The Indian government has started a new initiative called Green India, which focuses on a pollution-free environment and healthy living for all of us. Biodiesel has many advantages and is a good source for a greener environment. It helps in the reduction of the use of petroleum or diesel. It is also used as a cleaner for biofuel refineries.
